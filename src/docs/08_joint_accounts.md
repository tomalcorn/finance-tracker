# Joint Accounts

A joint account lets you and one other person share part of your finances
without merging everything. Shared bills, a shared pot, a holiday you are both
saving for — those live in the joint account. Everything else stays yours alone.

## What Is Shared And What Is Not

Everything you already have is private to you. Your partner cannot see your
personal spending, budgets, or bank accounts, and you cannot see theirs.

Only the things kept in the joint account are shared. You do not have to mark
anything as shared yourself — where you add something decides whether it is
private or joint.

Inside a joint account there is no "yours" and "mine". Both of you can add,
change, and delete anything in it, and neither of you has more control than the
other.

> **You can only be in one joint account at a time.** If you already share an
> account with someone, you cannot join a second one — for example a joint
> account with a partner and another with a housemate. Worth knowing before you
> set one up.

## Setting One Up

Joint accounts are not self-service yet. There is no button to create one or to
invite someone, so it has to be set up manually behind the scenes.

To get one, ask for a joint account to be created and say who should be on it.
Once you are both added, whatever is kept in the joint account becomes visible
to both of you.

## Seeing Each Other's Changes

When your partner changes something in the joint account, it appears for you the
next time the page loads. If you are expecting a change and cannot see it yet,
reload the page.

## What Is Not There Yet

Joint accounts are still being built out. At the moment:

- You cannot create one or add someone yourself — both are manual for now.
- There is no invite step, so nobody is asked to confirm before being added.
- Everyone on an account has the same rights; there is no owner or admin.
- There is no joint screen in the app yet, so shared data has nowhere to be
  viewed or entered.

If joint accounts turn out to be something people want to use properly, the
in-app version can be built out.
